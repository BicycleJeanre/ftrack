# Glossary

## 1.0 Glossary

A reference guide to common terms used throughout FTrack.

## 1.1 A

- **Account**: A place where money lives or is owed. Examples: checking account, savings account, credit card, loan. Accounts are fundamental to FTrack data model.
- **Account Type**: The classification of an account. Types include Asset, Liability, Income, and Expense. These help organize and categorize your financial information.
- **Actual Transaction**: A transaction that has already occurred in the real world. Used to track real spending and compare against planned amounts.

## 1.2 B

- **Balance**: The amount of money in an account at a specific point in time. Opening balance is your starting point; projected balance shows where you will be in the future.
- **Budget**: A type of scenario that enforces strict financial planning. Budget scenarios are designed to control spending and monitor expenses.

## 1.3 C

- **Compound Interest**: A periodic change that applies exponential growth, like investment returns. Interest earned also earns interest, creating accelerating growth.

## 1.4 D

- **Dual-Entry Transaction**: A transaction that involves two accounts. Money moves from one account to another, maintaining double-entry accounting principles.

## 1.5 E

- **Expense**: Money that flows out, such as rent, utilities, groceries, or subscriptions. Categorized as an Account Type for organizational purposes.

## 1.6 F

- **Flat Change**: A periodic change that adds or subtracts a fixed dollar amount each period. Example: salary increases by 100 dollars quarterly.
- **Forecast**: See Projection. The process of predicting future financial balances based on planned transactions and current accounts.

## 1.7 I

- **Income**: Money flowing in, such as salary, bonuses, or interest earned. Categorized as an Account Type for organization.

## 1.8 L

- **Liability**: Money you owe to others. Examples: credit cards, loans, mortgages. Tracked as an Account Type.

## 1.9 O

- **Opening Balance**: The starting balance of an account, as of the current date. This is the basis for all future projections.

## 1.10 P

- **Percentage Change**: A periodic change that applies a percentage adjustment each period. Example: salary grows 3 percent annually.
- **Periodic Change**: An automatic adjustment to a transaction amount that occurs at regular intervals. Used to model salary raises, inflation, and other gradual changes.
- **Planned Transaction**: An expected future transaction with a set amount and date. Can have recurrence and periodic changes.
- **Projection**: A forecast of future account balances based on current balances and planned transactions. Shows where your finances are headed.

## 1.11 Q

- **Quarterly**: Occurring every 3 months. A recurrence pattern for transactions that repeat 4 times per year.

## 1.12 R

- **Recurrence**: A repeating schedule for transactions. Patterns include weekly, monthly, quarterly, and yearly. Reduces data entry for repeating payments.

## 1.13 S

- **Scenario**: A complete financial plan or what-if situation. Contains its own accounts, transactions, and projections. Allows side-by-side comparison of different plans.

## 1.14 T

- **Transaction**: A movement of money between accounts. Can be planned, expected future, or actual, already occurred.

## 1.15 V

- **Variance**: The difference between planned and actual amounts. Used to refine budgets and identify spending patterns.

## 1.16 W

- **What-If Scenario**: A scenario designed to explore a specific change or decision. Example: what if I get a raise or what if I buy a car.
